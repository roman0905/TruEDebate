Abstract
Multimodal fake information increasingly threatens the Web ecosys-
tem’s trustworthiness and security, making improving detection
accuracy a critical scientific challenge. The limited information
interaction in traditional multimodal fake news detection meth-
ods fails to leverage semantic knowledge to model complex cross-
modal forgery patterns and global structural anomalies, restricting
the model’s capability. To address the issues, this paper proposes
a multimodal fake news detection method, SVPF-Net, that cen-
ters on semantic-driven visual enhancement and knowledge-aided
modality-priority fusion. For visual representation optimization,
we design a dual-feature extraction module and a dual-fusion en-
hancement module. A weighted fusion strategy is employed to
construct a structured visual representation that integrates the
semantics of local forgeries and global anomalies. Meanwhile, a
cross-attention mechanism enables bidirectional alignment and in-
teractive coupling between local and global image features, thereby
achieving effective complementarity between local forgery cues
and global anomaly patterns. For multimodal fusion, high-quality
textual semantic features and visual representations are integrated
via a modality-priority progressive fusion strategy that relies on
cross-attention. The integration enables robust cross-modal seman-
tic interaction and effectively enhances the efficiency of multimodal
feature fusion. Comprehensive experiments validate the optimal
performance of SVPF-Net and its ability to enhance interpretable
semantics, providing valuable support for the practical application
of reliable fake news detection.

Introduction
The Web has evolved into the core ecosystem for global information
exchange and value dissemination. In the era of all-media commu-
nication, fake news proliferates across social platforms, evolving
from purely textual forms to intricate combinations of text, im-
ages, and videos. The rapid spread of multimodal fake information
severely undermines user trust and poses a fundamental threat
to downstream applications that rely on structured, trustworthy
data. In this context, developing accurate detection models for mul-
timodal fake information in complex network environments has
become a critical research priority to ensure the trustworthiness
and resilience of the Web ecosystem [31, 43, 48]. Recent advances in
artificial intelligence, particularly deep learning, have substantially
improved multimodal fake news detection performance, with inte-
grating textual, visual, and sometimes auditory information [38].
These approaches typically leverage feature-level, decision-level,
or hybrid fusion mechanisms to improve classification accuracy
[16, 44].
However, existing multimodal fake news detection methods suf-
fer from two core limitations that are closely intertwined with

semantic understanding and knowledge integration. First, they
fail to effectively capture and associate two complementary, inter-
pretable semantic cues in fake images—local forgery traces and
global structural anomalies, due to the fragmented extraction of lo-

(ViT) [8, 11] can capture global structural semantics but often loses
fine-grained local details. Second, traditional multimodal fusion
frameworks often treat text and vision as equal or rely on simple
concatenation. This limited interaction fails to leverage semantic
knowledge for modeling complex cross-modal forgery patterns,
ultimately hindering the comprehensive detection of fake content.
To address the aforementioned issues, this paper proposes a
knowledge-enhanced multimodal fake news detection method,
SVPF-Net, based on semantic visual and priority fusion. With se-
mantic knowledge as the core driver, SVPF-Net improves both the
interpretability and discriminative power of fake information detec-
tion from two key perspectives: visual semantic enhancement and
cross-modal knowledge alignment. Specifically, in the text modal
feature extraction module, the latest pre-trained language model,
RoBERTa is adopted to endow text features with stronger seman-
tic consistency and knowledge interpretability. Second, the visual
feature extraction module incorporates a semantic enhancement
framework. This design strengthens the capture of interpretable
cues, encompassing both local forgery traces and global structural
anomalies within the fake images. Furthermore, to solve the prob-
lems of low interaction efficiency between modalities and unbal-
anced semantic fusion, this paper also designs a knowledge-guided
modality-priority progressive fusion strategy based on co-attention.
This strategy enables selective integration of cross-modal informa-
tion, ensuring robust semantic interaction while mitigating fusion
imbalance. The main contributions of this paper are summarized
as follows:
• We propose the semantic visual enhancement module, which
integrates the local perception of Convolutional Neural Net-
works (CNNs) with the global structural modeling of Vision
Transformers (ViTs).
• We propose a knowledge-guided priority fusion strategy
that assesses the relative importance of each modality. By
employing a collaborative attention mechanism, this strategy
establishes semantic dominance and facilitates progressive
fusion, ultimately enhancing the model’s capacity for cross-
modal knowledge interaction and reasoning.
• We conduct extensive experiments on publicly available real-
world social media datasets, demonstrating that SVPF-Net
significantly outperforms state-of-the-art methods in multi-
modal fake news detection.
2
Related Work
2.1
Single modal fake news detection
Supervised fake news detection methods based on text take the
textual information of news as input to identify fake news [29, 30].
Early approaches relied on distributed representations such as
Word2Vec [28] and shallow neural networks like CNN [21] and
RNN [27]. Although these methods could extract local semantic
patterns and sequence dependencies, they were limited by the defect
of fragmented contextual understanding [2, 13, 23, 50]. The emer-
gence of pre-trained language models has achieved a technological
breakthrough. BERT [7] improved global semantic representation
through large-scale self-supervised learning, while RoBERTa fur-
ther enhanced the ability to capture semantic details by optimizing
pre-training strategies, making it a preferred model for extracting
textual fake features. For fake information detection in the image
modality, the focus lies in identifying tampering traces and seman-
tic anomalies [24, 33]. In the stage of CNN-dominated local feature
extraction, CNN became the mainstream due to its strong local
feature learning capability [22]. VGGNet [35] deepened the feature
hierarchy by stacking small convolutional kernels, and ResNet [12]
addressed the gradient vanishing problem in deep networks using
residual connections. Inspired by the NLP field, ViT [8] segments
images into token sequences and realizes efficient global feature
modeling via the self-attention mechanism, demonstrating stronger
generalization ability in deepfake detection. However, ViT is less
capable than CNN in capturing subtle local tampering traces, suf-
fering from the defect of loss of local detail. In the exploration stage
of spatial-frequency domain feature fusion, researchers have begun
to pay attention to the supplementary value of frequency-domain
information. Qi et al. [33] proposed MVNN, which fuses frequency-
domain tampering traces captured by CNN with semantic features
in the pixel domain. Meanwhile, some studies have attempted to
combine the advantages of CNN and Transformer. Huang et al.
[15] proposed ViT-AMCNet, which integrates ViT with a convolu-
tional module embedded with an attention mechanism. Efficient
ViT directly adopts the architecture of CNN feature extraction com-
bined with Transformer encoding, initially realizing the synergy
between local and global features. However, these methods are
mostly applied to general image classification tasks and lack spe-
cialized optimization for manipulating features targeted at detecting
fake news.
2.2
Multi-modal Fake News Detection
The landscape of misinformation detection has evolved from rudi-
mentary unimodal analysis to a more sophisticated stage of multi-
modal fusion [1, 9, 19, 47]. Gao et al. [10] put forward a dynamic
multi-modal feature fusion method that enables the alternating
transfer of dynamic information between visual and linguistic
modalities. Huang et al. [14] proposed a deep multi-modal attention
fusion model (DMAF) that exploits distinguishing features and in-
herent correlations between visual and semantic content. SpotFake
[37], simple yet effective, independently transforms features from
text and images and finally uses a fully connected neural network
to classify the news as real or fake. The same authors later proposed
SpotFake+, which incorporates multi-modal semantic information
and transfer learning to capture the semantic and contextual in-
formation present in news texts and their corresponding images
for better fake news detection accuracy [36]. Wu et al. [42] fur-
ther stacked multiple co-attention layers to fuse spatial-domain,
frequency-domain, and text features, enhancing the model’s ca-
pability to learn interdependencies across features. The HMCAN
model [34] combines hierarchical semantics with the Co-attention
Mechanism (CA) to capture rich linguistic information. Choi et

improvement of detection accuracy and robustness.

3.2
Feature fusion module
3.2.1
Co-Attention mechanism module. We adopt the same
Co-Attention mechanism (CA) as Jin et al. [18] to enhance the
information exchange between different modal features. The CA
mechanism achieves dynamic correlation of cross-modal informa-
tion using the features of one modality as Q and the features of
another modality as K and V.
3.2.2

cess of multimodal feature fusion, a single feature interaction is
difficult to fully capture cross-modal information. Therefore, we
adopt a hierarchical progressive fusion strategy, gradually intro-

domain, and image frequency domain based on the importance of
modal information.

Experiments
4.1
Experimental Setup
This subsection introduces the datasets, comparison methods, hy-
perparameter settings, and evaluation metrics.
4.1.1
Datasets. We focus on multimodal fake information detec-
tion, mainly using text and image data. We adopted two commonly
used real-world datasets, one from the Chinese Weibo dataset [18]
and the other from the English Pheme dataset [51], to verify the
performance of SVPF-Net. They correspond to fake news detection
tasks in both Chinese and English text scenarios. After prepro-
cessing, tweets containing videos or lacking text and images were
removed, and the baseline testing data partitioning scheme was
used.
4.1.2
Baselines. To comprehensively verify the effectiveness of
the proposed method, we selected representative baseline models
for comparative experiments. For the Weibo dataset, we selected
three unimodal models, including Text, Spatial, and Freq, which
are based on the MCAN [42] model framework and only retain the
unimodal results obtained from the text modality, image spatial
modality, and image frequency domain modality feature informa-
tion, respectively. Furthermore, we also introduce a representative
unimodal graph neural network model, TextGCN [45]. Regarding
multimodal baselines, we have selected several representative mod-
els from recent years, including NeuralTalk [39], att-RNN [18],
EANN [41], MVAE [20], SpotFake [37], HMCAN [34], MCAN [42],
CAFE [4], BMR [46] and MVML [6]. They cover various fusion
strategies such as feature layer fusion, decision layer fusion, and
attention collaborative fusion to compare the superiority of the
fusion strategy proposed in this paper. For the Pheme dataset, se-
lect models that are clearly based on the Pheme dataset and can
obtain complete performance indicators from the 14 models men-
tioned above as comparison objects to ensure the reliability and
comparability of experimental results.
4.1.3
Parameter Settings. We set the maximum text length to
160 for the Weibo dataset and 60 for the Pheme dataset. The em-
bedding dimension model_dim for each modality is set to 256, the
number of layers 𝑚for the Cross-Attention (CA) layer is 2, and the
ffn_dim is 2048. The hidden layer dimension in the classification
head is 16. For the Weibo dataset, we load the RoBERTa_zh_L12
model to acquire basic understanding of Chinese corpora; for the
Pheme dataset, we load the RoBERTa-base model pre-trained on
large-scale English corpora. The Adabelief optimizer and Adam
optimizer are used for the Weibo and Pheme datasets, respectively.
The learning rate and weight_decay are adjusted according to the
number of trainable parameters. We train the model for 50 epochs
on the Weibo dataset and 100 epochs on the Pheme dataset, with
an early stopping mechanism adopted to prevent overfitting during
model training.
4.1.4
Evaluation Metrics. Fake news detection is essentially a
binary classification task. We selected four classification evaluation
metrics, namely Accuracy, Precision, Recall and F1-Score. These

4.2
Performance Comparison
To validate the effectiveness and superiority of our proposed model,
SVPF-Net, we conducted comprehensive comparative experiments
with several baseline methods on the Weibo and Pheme datasets.
The detailed results are presented in Table 1. The analysis shows
that our model achieves state-of-the-art performance on both datasets.
Specifically, on the Weibo dataset, SVPF-Net achieved an accuracy
of 0.912 and a fake news F1-score of 0.911. Notably, its recall reached
0.930, consistently surpassing strong baselines such as MCAN and
MVML. This performance advantage is even more pronounced on
the Pheme dataset, where our model delivered a top accuracy of
0.915 and an F1-score of 0.850. Compared to the second-best model,
MVML, SVPF-Net improved the accuracy by 2.4%. These consistent
and significant improvements across diverse datasets validate that
the proposed semantic visual enhancement and modality-priority
progressive fusion strategy are highly effective for multimodal fake
news detection.
4.3
Ablation Study
4.3.1
Component Contribution Analysis. To assess each com-
ponent’s contribution, we conducted an ablation study, with results
presented in Table 2. The analysis confirms that each module is
indispensable for the model’s final performance. The text modality
acts as the semantic backbone, as its removal leads to the most
significant degradation, causing the accuracy to drop by 18% on
Weibo and 20.5% on Pheme. Our dual-branch visual feature extrac-
tion, which combines a CNN for local traces and a Transformer
for global context, is also validated; removing either component
demonstrably harms performance, confirming their complemen-
tary nature in building a comprehensive visual representation. Most
critically, the Cross-Attention (CA) fusion mechanism proves to be
essential. Disabling this module results in a severe performance
decline, with accuracy dropping by 2.1% on Weibo and 4.4% on
Pheme. This demonstrates that beyond powerful feature extraction,
an effective mechanism to model intricate cross-modal interactions
is paramount to the model’s success.

Analysis of Fusion Method. To validate our choice of
fusion strategy, we compared our model’s concatenation fusion
with a simpler add fusion method, as detailed in Table 3. The results
demonstrate that concatenation fusion consistently outperforms the
alternative on both datasets, achieving the highest accuracy of 0.912
on Weibo and 0.915 on Pheme. This indicates that concatenating
feature vectors from different fusion stages is more effective at
preserving their distinct and complementary information, leading
to a more robust final representation for the detection task.

performance on both datasets, achieving a top accuracy of 0.912 on

that initializing the fusion process with the semantically rich text
modality provides a robust context that most effectively guides the
subsequent integration of visual information.
4.4
Discussion
The superior performance of our model, validated across both
datasets, is attributable to two principal innovations: the semantic
visual enhancement module and the knowledge-guided priority
fusion strategy. The ablation results presented in Table 2 confirm
that our dual-branch visual extractor, which synergizes a CNN’s
ability to capture local manipulation artifacts with a Transformer’s
capacity for global semantic reasoning, creates a far more compre-
hensive visual representation than either architecture alone. This
enhanced visual understanding is then effectively integrated using
our modality-priority fusion approach. As demonstrated by the
fusion order study in Table 4, the success of the Text →Visual
→Frequency sequence validates our hypothesis that using the
text modality as a semantic anchor provides crucial context, guid-
ing the subsequent fusion of visual features. The CA mechanism
facilitates this hierarchical integration, enabling a deeper cross-
modal interaction that results in more discriminative features, as
visually evidenced by the clear class separation in Figure 2. This
moves beyond simple feature concatenation to a more effective,
context-aware fusion process.
5
Conclusion
In this paper, we address the critical challenges of inadequate visual
representation and inefficient modality fusion in multimodal fake
news detection. To this end, we propose a novel detection method,
SVPF-Net, centered on semantic-driven visual enhancement and a
knowledge-aided modality-priority fusion strategy. Our core con-
tributions are twofold: (1) a semantic visual enhancement module
that synergistically combines the local-feature extraction power of
CNNs with the global context modeling of Transformers, yielding a
rich and structured visual representation that captures interpretable
semantic cues; and (2) a knowledge-guided priority fusion strategy
that leverages a hierarchical Co-Attention mechanism to realize
progressive modality fusion. Specifically, this strategy takes textual
features as a semantic anchor, capitalizing on the strong semantic
consistency of text to guide the selective integration of visual infor-
mation, effectively mitigating inter-modal interaction inefficiencies
and semantic fusion imbalances. Extensive experiments on the
real-world datasets, including Weibo and Pheme demonstrate that
SVPF-Net significantly outperforms existing state-of-the-art meth-
ods. The results validate SVPF-Net’s ability to effectively model
complex cross-modal forgery patterns, thereby providing a more
accurate and robust solution for ensuring the trustworthiness of
the Web ecosystem.
In future work, we plan to incorporate a broader range of het-
erogeneous signals, such as video sequences and social network
propagation graphs, to construct a more comprehensive and holistic
detection framework. Furthermore, we will explore model compres-
sion and lightweight architectural designs, aiming to significantly
reduce computational overhead and resource consumption while
maintaining high detection accuracy.

Appendix A: Detailed Specifications of Baseline
Models
To validate the performance of SVPF-Net, we compare it against a
wide range of state-of-the-art baselines. These models are catego-
rized based on their architectural designs and modality processing
strategies.
A.1 Unimodal Baselines
To evaluate the contribution of individual modalities and the effec-
tiveness of graph-based text modeling, we consider the following
unimodal baselines:
• Text, Spatial, and Freq: These are unimodal variants de-
rived from the MCAN framework [42] used for ablation
study, retaining only the textual branch, spatial visual branch,
or frequency-domain visual branch, respectively.
• TextGCN [45]: A representative graph-based text classifica-
tion model that builds a heterogeneous corpus graph with
word and document nodes. It utilizes Graph Convolutional
Networks (GCN) to capture global word co-occurrence and
learn discriminative embeddings.
A.2 Multimodal Baselines
The following models represent diverse fusion paradigms, ranging
from early generative approaches to modern cross-modal attention
networks:
(1) NeuralTalk [39]: A generative model based on a deep re-
current architecture that employs a CNN for visual feature
extraction and an LSTM for natural language generation,
mapping images directly to textual descriptions.
(2) att-RNN [18]: Utilizes an LSTM to fuse textual and social-
context features with image features extracted by VGG-19,
while employing an attention mechanism for dynamic visual-
text alignment.
(3) EANN [41]: Extracts textual features via Text-CNN and vi-
sual features via VGG-19, jointly training a fake news detec-
tor to learn event-invariant representations.
(4) MVAE [20]: Combines Bi-LSTM text features and VGG-19
visual features; learns shared cross-modal latent represen-
tations through joint optimization of reconstruction and
KL-divergence losses.
(5) SpotFake [37]: Employs BERT for text and VGG-19 for im-
ages, concatenating both feature vectors for fake news clas-
sification via fully connected layers.
(6) HMCAN [34]: Uses BERT to extract textual features and
ResNet-50 to extract regional visual features. A hierarchical
multimodal context attention network fuses intra- and inter-
modal relations, followed by hierarchical encoding for text
semantics and final classification.
(7) MCAN [42]: Extracts spatial features using VGG-19, frequency-
domain features via CNN, and textual features via BERT.
Multi-layer co-attention modules progressively fuse visual
and textual representations to capture inter-modal depen-
dencies.
(8) CAFE [4]: Extracts textual features via BERT and visual
features via ResNet, performs cross-modal alignment and
ambiguity learning using KL divergence, and adaptively ag-
gregates uni- and cross-modal representations for classifica-
tion.
(9) BMR [46]: Integrates an improved mixture-of-experts mech-
anism to fuse tri-view representations (text, image modality,
and image semantics), performing disentanglement and re-
weighting through consistency learning to guide fake news
detection.
(10) SAFE [49]: The method employs Text-CNN to extract textual
features and utilizes a modified VGG-19 for visual feature
extraction. Subsequently, the cosine similarity between the
textual and visual features is calculated to quantify the cross-
modal consistency of the news content.
(11) MVML [6]: The method first employs BERT and ResNet/Faster
R-CNN to extract features from text, image scenes, and ob-
jects, respectively. Subsequently, it captures cross-modal se-
mantic relationships from both global and local perspectives
by constructing text-scene and text-object heterogeneous
graphs and applying graph attention-based reasoning. Fi-
nally, the model utilizes a cross-view attention mechanism
for feature fusion and incorporates a mutual learning mech-
anism based on KL divergence.
